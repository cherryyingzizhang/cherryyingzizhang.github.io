Background

Advanced AI-controlled robots are still not adaptive nor intelligent enough right now to do general tasks effectively. The company, Jinki, attempts to circumvent this issue by having the human operator at the forefront as the sole controller of the robot and all of its tasks. Humans are better at general decision making and can adapt better to novel situations than even the latest developed AIs. This type of control system, with a human operator controlling everything, is called a ‘master-slave system’.

Problem

Jinki, the sponsor of this project, has created and patented a novel feedback system called ‘force projecting bilateral control’ and wants to compare it with other known and established master-slave control methods for the purposes of demonstrating the uniqueness and efficacy of their control system to relevant stakeholders.

Solution

Group 46 will design two robotic arms - one as the master and the other as the slave - evaluate the different master-slave control designs.

Mechanical

Two identical robotic arms will be made where one is the master and the other is the slave. Each arm has four rigid links that are connected in a parallelogram shape. Two motors will actuate two of the links, and the other two links will move passively. This configuration provides stability as both motors will be connected to the arm at the same stable base. The human operator can then move the master (or even the slave) at the end effector.

Electrical

Each robotic arm will have two DC motors actuating them, and a capacitative force sensor to measure the Mz torque and the Fx and Fy forces applied at the end effector by the human operator. The entire system will be controlled via a cRIO-9047 embedded controller.

Control

Several control designs will be implemented and compared with each other. Here are a few:

対称型　position-symmetric type
力逆送型　force-reflecting type
力帰還型　force-reflecting servo type
力順送型　force-projecting type
並列型　parallel type
インピーダンス制御型　impedance control type
加速度基準型　acceleration-based type
